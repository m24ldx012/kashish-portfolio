SealSafe Diapers

Redesign of diapers for clean, discrete and dignified defecation

Research Time
7 hrs
contextual inquiry
Iterations
100+
SCAMPER concepts

TL; DR

We deep dived into core issues of bedridden patients with no mobility/ conscious bowel movement regarding their experience with defecation and redesigned diapers to be self-sealing, ensuring hygiene for care givers, dignity for patients and a non-messy experience.

My Role

As part of the team, I wore multiple hats in the process. During the discover stage, I conducted the interviews while my peers noted. I was actively involved in generating insights, speaking to doctors from rural hospitals as well as care givers of different strata of the society. Scampered 100+ iterations and 8 dirty prototypes. The final presentation included the renders created by me as well as the material design.

Why is defecation even an issue in the case of bedridden patients with no mobility?

Patients with faecal incontinence are 22x more likely to develop infections, pressure ulcers (bedsores) and fatality than those without amongst bedridden patients.

But.. aren’t there existing products for bedridden patients?

Well, to find out, my team and I spent 7+ hours, contextual inquiry, interviewing patients, caregivers and hospital staff at AIIMS Jodhpur, and here’s what we found out:

Here’s what they told us—

At an average, a patient requires change of diapers every 6 hours. But in practice, the ground reality based on our study said 75% caregivers do not change diapers any more than twice every 24 hours.

So what does it mean for the user? And why???

Affinity Mapping

Image Placeholder: Affinity Mapping Board

To understand the gaps we pinned 200+ insights across our blackboards and grouped them in order to generate 6 key insights

From there, we turned every detailed insight into actions that could be taken to mitigate the barriers/ impediments that kept from achieving the desired experience.

And through an Action Priority Matrix (Urgent Important Matrix), we concluded to the Action statement—

A product that caters to immobile/ unconscious patients to defecate with minimal contact, mitigates infection risks, promotes hygiene and aids safe disposal while intuitive on how it shall be used or disposed.

From an informed assumption of the demographic at AIIMS Jodhpur’s ICU wards, we chose a persona to be the sixth member of our team— Our user persona Rukmini.

Image Placeholder: Persona / Empathy Map (Rukmini)

Rationale

Diapers are a direct contact product and the only existing solution for bedridden patients with no mobility/ no bowel control. In such cases, the biggest concerns are risk of infections, bedsores from constant friction and unsanitary disposal. It may involve direct contact of care givers with the fecal matter and compromise their health, as well as tends to be emotionally difficult to cope with for both the patient and care giver.

How might we design sanitary defecation aid...

...for innovating a product for Rukimi that caters to immobile/ unconscious patients/ patients without bowel control to defecate with minimal contact, mitigates infection risks, promotes hygiene and aids safe disposal while providing clear intuitive design on how it shall be used or disposed?

So what did we ideate from a statement to a product???

After rapidly ideating 100+ concepts and SCAMPERed through them to see what we can substitute, combine, adapt, modify, put to another use, eliminate and reverse.

Image Placeholder: Ideation Sketches / SCAMPER

We followed this with making 10+ prototypes, to understand scare, ergonomics and form.

What we loved but didnt go ahead with

The concept was to make an adhesive stick-on that is attached to the rectal lining and has a defecation pouch attached to its outer side that can be discarded after every discharge. Pros: Cost effective, easy disposal Cons: Uncomfortable, risk of infection

So.. WHAT DID WE CREATE?

Image Placeholder: Final Product Renders (SealSafe Diaper)

We went with the prototype with least learning curve for the care giver that was sanitary, discreet and offered east disposal.

We made SealSafe, a two layered self sealing diaper that locks faecal matter in between the layers. It has a drawstring mechanism for after use to seal the contents without risk of spillage.

Its designed for hygienic disposal with a drawstring mechanism, simplifying the process for caregivers while minimizing contamination risk.

A stick-on adhesive patch near the rectal area creates a clean passage for waste, preventing it from sticking to the skin. It’s non-invasive and comfortable.

Image Placeholder: Mechanism Detail / Layers

With minimal alteration to current adult diaper design, the design is meant to ensure easy adoption and comfort. The design also ensures sealing off faecal matter away from genitals for maximum hygiene. The top absorbent layer is for genital sweat and discharge while the core pulp and liquid gel rapidly dries and locks the faecal matter. Thus ensure genital health as well as moisture control.

Post use, as the drawstring successfully seals off the faecal matter between the absorbent layers, the care giver can fold and wrap the diaper in itself without spillage and easy disposal.

Our Trade-offs

While designing the SealSafe diapers, our priority was dignity, hygiene and safe disposal. While the design would make the cost higher with an additional layer, it ensures utmost priority to the patient’s health and caregiver’s comfort.

End note

The project taught me the complexity of hospital environments first hand and to empathize in the critical context.
