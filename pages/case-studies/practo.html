TLDR — Executive Summary

The Shift: Instead of a design summary, this is your investment thesis.

The Narrative: Practo is bleeding value on both sides of the marketplace. Patients treat it as a disposable booking link; clinics view it as an expensive lead generator that doesn't solve their operational chaos.

The Solution: A dual-pronged AI engine. For clinics: Predictive demand forecasting to maximize slot yield. For patients: Lifecycle intelligence that shifts the model from "sick-care booking" to "preventative health management."

01 — Problem Framing & The Business Reality

Why is Practo losing the ecosystem war?

Clinics are churning to dedicated SaaS tools because Practo only fills the top of the funnel. The booking fee model is a race to the bottom. To survive, Practo must own the clinic's operational infrastructure and the patient's continuous health data.

The "So What?"

The core business viability depends on moving from transactional leads to infrastructure stickiness.

02 — Two-Sided Market & Ecosystem Mapping

Instead of just mapping the UI workflow, map the flow of data and money.

Supply Side

Clinics & Hospitals

Facing high no-show rates, unpredictable daily demand, and fragmented records. They need yield management (like airlines).

Demand Side

The Patients

High friction for follow-ups, zero post-consultation engagement, treating healthcare reactively.

03 — Strategic User Segmentation

Defining the high-value segments

Clinic Segment A

Solo Practitioners

Needs plug-and-play AI to act as their digital receptionist and recall system.

Clinic Segment B

Multi-specialty Polyclinics

Needs heavy demand forecasting to allocate staff and optimize expensive machine time (MRI/X-ray).

Patient Segment A

The Chronic Patient (High LTV)

Needs continuous monitoring, automated prescription refills, and proactive check-ins.

Patient Segment B

Acute / Transactional Patient

Needs fast triage and outcome-based doctor matching.

04 — North Star & Guardrail Metrics

Shift from task-completion times to business viability and retention.

North Star

Ecosystem Retention Rate

% of patients returning to the same clinic via Practo for follow-ups/diagnostics within 6 months.

Secondary (Clinic)

Slot Utilization Rate

Target: Increase from ~60% to 85% using predictive overbooking and waitlist automation.

Secondary (Patient)

Lifecycle Engagement Score

DAU/MAU for non-booking actions like viewing lab reports or AI health nudges.

Guardrail

Clinic Churn Rate

Ensure new AI features don't overwhelm staff or increase costs without immediate ROI.

05 — AI Product Strategy & Core Loops

Designing the algorithm's job, not just the screen.

Feature 1: Predictive Demand

Using historical data and seasonality to predict no-shows and automatically adjust scheduling buffers.

Feature 2: Lifecycle Engine

Post-consultation automated workflows (e.g., pre-diabetes 3-month engagement loop with diet nudges and reminders).

Feature 3: Outcome-Based Matching

Shifting search from "who is available" to "who has the best historical outcomes for specific symptoms."

06 — Prioritization (The PM Crucible)

The Hard Choice: What are we not building?

Using RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) to separate noise from signal.

"We deprioritized AI symptom checker chatbots (high legal risk, low trust) in favor of AI clinic slot optimization (immediate revenue impact, low clinical risk)."
